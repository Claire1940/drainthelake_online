<Section icon={Swords} title="Bucket Upgrades and Collection Efficiency">

Your bucket is the most important piece of **drain the lake equipment** you will interact with. In the early game, your bucket capacity is severely limited, forcing you to make dozens of trips between the water and the drain. To optimize this, you must understand the relationship between capacity and speed.

Upgrading your bucket involves spending tokens earned from the drainage process. However, capacity isn't the only factor. Collection efficiency—how fast the bucket fills when you are in the water—is equally important. If you have a massive bucket but a slow fill rate, you will spend too much time standing still, which reduces your overall tokens-per-hour (TPH).

<Callout type="warning" title="The Travel Time Bottleneck">
The biggest mistake new players make is ignoring movement speed. As the lake drains, the water line recedes further from the drain point. If your bucket capacity is low, you will spend 80% of your time walking and only 20% actually draining water.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Step-by-Step Farming Route">

To maximize the effectiveness of your **drain the lake equipment**, you need a consistent farming route. Efficiency is measured by how quickly you can complete a full "Loop." A loop consists of the following five steps:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify the Shortest Path">
    Locate the nearest point of water relative to the drain. In the early game, this is usually directly in front of the spawn, but as the lake empties, you may need to find side-paths or ramps.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Optimize Collection Time">
    Submerge your bucket and wait for the fill bar. Use any "Instant Fill" procs from your Skill Tree to immediately turn back toward the drain.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Sprint to the Drain">
    Use your stamina to sprint back. If your bucket is heavy (high tier), your movement speed may be slightly reduced, making speed upgrades even more valuable.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Deposit">
    Stand within the drain zone until the bucket is empty. Do not leave early, or you will lose the remaining water and potential tokens.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Reinvest and Repeat">
    Check your token balance. If you can afford the next tier of the Skill Tree or a bucket upgrade, purchase it immediately to improve the next loop.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Optimization Note">
Experienced players often wait to buy upgrades until they can afford two at once. This "leapfrogging" strategy can sometimes be faster than buying incremental 5% boosts that don't significantly change the number of trips required.
</Callout>
