<Section icon={Target} title="Bucket Upgrades and Equipment Scaling">

As you gather more tokens, your primary focus should be the evolution of your equipment. The bucket isn't just a static item; it features several upgradeable stats that directly impact your "Tokens Per Hour" (TPH) ratio. Understanding which stats to prioritize will save you hours of unnecessary grinding.

Efficiency in Drain the Lake is a balance between how much you can carry and how fast you can move it. If your capacity is too high but your movement speed is low, you waste time traveling. Conversely, if you are fast but have a tiny bucket, you waste time constantly returning to the drain.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Skill Tree: Permanent Item Enhancements">

The Skill Tree is where you turn your temporary gains into permanent power. Unlike the bucket, which you upgrade linearly, the Skill Tree allows for "builds" that can favor different playstyles. Every node in the tree acts as a modifier for your **drain the lake items**, making them more effective in specific environments.

There are three main branches in the current 2026 meta: Collection, Economy, and Utility. Balancing these branches is the key to reaching the bottom of the lake without hitting a progression wall.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Economy Foundations">
    Invest your first 500 tokens into the **Token Multiplier** node. This increases the value of every liter of water you drain, effectively speeding up all future purchases.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Collection Efficiency">
    Unlock the **Quick-Fill** ability. This reduces the time it takes to fill your bucket by 30%, which is vital when you are dodging obstacles in deeper water.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Depth Resistance">
    As you go deeper, the water pressure increases. Unlock the **Pressure Suit** node to maintain your movement speed even at the lowest points of the lake.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Mastery Nodes">
    Finally, aim for the **Auto-Drain** mastery. This allows a portion of your water to be converted to tokens automatically, reducing the need to return to the surface as frequently.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Skill Branch | Key Node | Effect | Priority |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Collection** | Suction Power | Faster water gathering | High |
| **Economy** | Interest Rate | Passive token gain | Medium |
| **Utility** | Oxygen Tank | Longer underwater duration | Critical |
| **Exploration** | Sonar | Highlights hidden treasures | Low |

<Callout type="tip" title="Respec Strategy">
If you find that your progression has stalled, check if you have too many points in 'Exploration'. In the early game, 'Economy' and 'Collection' provide much higher returns on investment.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Optimization Tips for Farming Tokens">

To maximize your efficiency with **drain the lake items**, you must optimize your route. Farming tokens is not just about having the best bucket; it's about minimizing "dead time"—the time spent neither collecting nor draining water.

Experienced players use a technique called "Short-Looping" in the early game and "Depth-Pushing" in the late game. By understanding the physics of the water drain, you can position yourself to minimize the walking distance between the water source and the collection point.

| Farming Method | Recommended Tier | Tokens Per Minute | Effort Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Shoreline Scooping** | Tier 1-2 | 50 - 150 | Low |
| **Mid-Depth Cycling** | Tier 3 | 400 - 800 | Medium |
| **Deep-Sea Dredging** | Tier 4-5 | 2,000+ | High |
| **AFK Passive Gain** | Any (with nodes) | 100 (Static) | None |

Current top-tier players suggest focusing entirely on **Token Efficiency** nodes until you reach 10,000 total tokens. This "greedy" start allows you to bypass the mid-game struggle entirely by jumping straight to the Gold Bucket tier.

Always remember that the lake is dynamic. As the water level drops, the environment changes. Items that were once deep underwater might become accessible on dry land, and new paths will open up. Keep a close eye on the "Drain Percentage" UI element to know when to shift your farming location.
