Finding every single chest requires a keen eye and proper preparation. Below is a detailed breakdown of the exact locations where chests spawn across the different depth zones of the lake.

### Tier 1: Shallow Zone Chests (0m - 50m)
These chests are perfect for beginners who are just starting their water-draining journey. They spawn near the primary shoreline and the initial draining station.
- **The Pier Underbelly**: Tucked directly beneath the wooden starting pier. Walk off the side of the platform and look backward.
- **The Draining Pipe Exit**: Located behind the large metal grate where your collected water is poured.
- **Sandy Alcove**: Follow the right-hand shoreline from the spawn point until you hit a rocky wall. The chest sits behind a small boulder.

### Tier 2: Submerged Zone Chests (51m - 150m)
As you venture deeper into the lake, the terrain becomes more uneven, and chests are hidden inside underwater caves.
- **The Sunken Rowboat**: Inside the decaying hull of an old wooden rowboat resting on a sandy ridge.
- **Kelpy Crevice**: Hidden behind a thick patch of tall, green seaweed. You must walk directly through the seaweed to reveal the chest.
- **Rocky Archway**: Resting right on top of a natural stone archway that connects two underwater cliffs.

### Tier 3: Deep-Sea Zone Chests (151m - 300m)
These locations are highly dangerous due to the depth and travel time. Proper pathing is required to avoid running out of oxygen or stamina.
- **The Coral Cave**: Located inside a vibrant, glowing coral cave on the eastern side of the lake floor.
- **Hydrothermal Vent**: Positioned on a ledge directly above an active underwater steam vent.
- **The Lost Anchor**: Sitting next to a massive rusted anchor embedded in a deep trench.
